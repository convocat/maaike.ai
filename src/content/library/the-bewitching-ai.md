A Wittgensteinian analysis showing LLMs lack the constancy required for genuine dialogue. Bottazzi and Ferrario argue that communication requires a stable speaker with commitments that persist across time. LLMs have no such constancy: each response is generated fresh, with no memory of commitment and no stake in what was said before. The bewitchment is that it looks like conversation while the conditions for conversation are absent.

Published 2025 in Philosophy & Technology.
