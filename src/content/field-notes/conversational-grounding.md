Conversational grounding is the process by which participants in a conversation establish mutual understanding. It was first described by Clark and Brennan (1991).

## Key principles

1. **Common ground** — the shared knowledge and beliefs of the participants
2. **Grounding criterion** — participants seek evidence that they've been understood
3. **Least collaborative effort** — people try to minimise the total effort in communication

## Application to AI

When working with an LLM, grounding happens differently. The model doesn't truly "understand" — but it can signal understanding through paraphrasing, asking clarifying questions, and building on previous context.

This connects to [[prompt-scaffolding]] — structuring prompts to progressively build shared context.
