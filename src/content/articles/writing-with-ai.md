Writing with AI is not the same as having AI write for you. The distinction matters.

When I use an AI as a writing tool, I'm still the one making decisions about structure, argument, and voice. The AI helps me explore possibilities, overcome blocks, and refine expression.

## What AI is good at

- **Brainstorming** — generating options to choose from
- **Restructuring** — suggesting alternative ways to organise ideas
- **Editing** — catching inconsistencies and unclear passages
- **Research** — surfacing relevant information quickly

## What AI is not good at

- **Having opinions** — it reflects patterns, not genuine perspectives
- **Making judgements** — it can't decide what's worth writing about
- **Original insight** — the novel connections still come from the human

The most productive collaboration happens when you treat the AI as a thinking partner, not an author.
